Trekking package to Nomad Riverside Lodge 3 days - 2 nights

Climbing in the most remote mountainous region in the Sa Pa area is equivalent to a real adventure that offers a great opportunity to experience the life of ethnic minorities in the mountains. Along the way, you will meet many different ethnic groups, each with their own language and costumes. Overnight at Nomad Riverside Lodge. Your trip starts from Hanoi to Sapa by the luxurious Nomad Mountain Express, which is an 8-seat limousine with modern, spacious and smooth interior design, ensuring you will have a trip comfortable.

Schedule


Tour code: NS03

Activities: Mountain climbing

Time: 3 days 2 night - Can stay for 1 or more nights.


Day 1: From Hanoi to the mountains where the majority of the Red Dao people live


From 6:30 to 7:00 am, Nomad Mountain Express will pick you up from your hotel in Hanoi and travel for nearly 5 hours to Thanh Phu Bridge, then go a short distance to Nam Cang village. Once you arrive, you will walk across the village, cross a small bridge and check in at Nomad Riverside Lodge. Your lunch is served at this homestay itself. The afternoon is an ideal time to visit the Red Dao village. Nam Cang is located in the most remote area of ​​Sa Pa and the Red Dao women always wear the most colorful and diverse clothes among Vietnam's ethnic minority communities. The Red Dao are very good at preparing herbal bath water, planting medicinal plants and embroidery. Your guide will accompany you to explore around the village and learn how the villagers make writing paper from rice, manipulating silver and embroidery. Back at your homestay, you might want to try a herbal bath. The ingredients for the bath water are made from various herbs, which are collected by the Red Dao women in the forest next to the homestay. You will also enjoy a cozy dinner here.

Additional activity:

Accompany a native woman to choose her own herbal mix and spend the evening enjoying the bath. Herbal bath and tour must be booked and paid in advance by yourself.


Day 2: A climbing day to neighboring villages of ethnic minorities


Enjoy breakfast with a cup of hot tea before starting your tour. Your climb begins with a short walk up the small village of the Green H’mong. The Green H’mong people mainly live on the other side of the Hoang Lien Son mountain range and there are only 2 small villages in the Sapa valley. Then you go downhill to the Red Dao village in Nam Nhiu, which has beautiful views of high mountains, waterfalls and valleys. You enjoy lunch at a beautiful place to stop. In the afternoon, you will turn into a small dirt road and soon be overwhelmed by the beauty of the terraced fields looming over the bamboo huts that farmers will be in the harvest season. Crossing the fields and a beautiful stream, you will walk through a quiet road before returning to Nam Cang village. This is a great time to drink a cold beer or simply relax by the stream in front of the resort.


Day 3: Walking to explore the pristine nature. Back to Hanoi


In the morning, you will walk into the forest to explore the beautiful natural scenery. You will meet locals walking on bare feet. The higher you go, the more you will be attracted by nature. Along the trail, you will come across a variety of plants, bamboo and herbs. Cardamom is grown in this area, which has a spicy flavor and is used to cook traditional dishes. Listening to the sounds of the mountains and birds, you will find a clear stream flowing from the mountains. This is an interesting stop for you to enjoy nature, soak your feet in the cool water. You will spend some time to explore the surrounding forest before returning. Leaving the mountain, you return to the village at noon. You may want to swim at the stream if weather permits before enjoying lunch. At 14:50, the bus will pick you up to Thanh Phu Bridge, board the Nomad Mountain Express at 15:30 and return to your hotel in Hanoi.

Caution:

- Nomad Mountain Express can only pick up and drop off passengers in Hanoi Old Quarter and Tay Ho District.
- Transportation and excursion trips are based on group coupling.
